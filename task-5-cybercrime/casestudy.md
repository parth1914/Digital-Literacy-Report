The "Quick KYC" Trap

Imagine a student named Rohan who gets a call while he’s busy with classes. The person on the phone says they are from his bank and that his account will be blocked today unless he updates his KYC immediately. To "help" him, they send a QR code over WhatsApp. They tell Rohan to scan it and enter his UPI PIN just to "verify" that the link works. 

Because he’s in a hurry, Rohan scans the code and enters his PIN. Within seconds, 20,000 rupees are gone from his account. He didn't realize that you never need a PIN to receive money, only to send it. This is a classic social engineering trick where scammers use fear and urgency to make people stop thinking clearly. For a college student, losing that much money is a huge hit, and since it was "authorized" with a PIN, getting it back from the bank is nearly impossible. It’s a tough lesson on why we should never rush into digital transactions.
